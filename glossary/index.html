Glossary & Terms
Pressure Classes
LPLow Pressure — 4″ to 12″ WC.
IPIntermediate Pressure — 1 to 5 PSIG.
MPMedium Pressure — 2 to 15 PSIG.
HPHigh Pressure — 15 to 99 PSIG.
XHPExtra High Pressure — 125 to 350 PSIG.
Pipe Materials
PEPolyethylene — plastic gas pipe; the standard for new mains & services.
STSteel — used for higher-pressure mains, services & risers.
CI / WICast Iron / Wrought Iron — legacy main material targeted for replacement.
Charging Classifications
CapitalNew plant or betterment charged to a capital project (Main Account).
O&MOperations & Maintenance — expense work (repair / replace in kind).
RetirementRemoval or abandonment of existing facilities (CI/WI, ST, or PE).
Service & Restoration Suffixes
1 / 2 / 3Service territory length: 1 = Manhattan (≤30′) · 2 = Queens / Bronx (≤50′) · 3 = Westchester (≤65′).
SSteel service (vs. the default plastic).
I / III = within (≤) territory length · II = beyond (>) territory length.
A / R / IRestoration (Micro): A = installation only · R = removal + installation · I = incremental (stacks with other suffixes).
Common Acronyms
MAOPMaximum Allowable Operating Pressure — the highest pressure a segment may be operated at.
WCWater Column (inches) — the unit used to measure low-pressure gas.
WO / WRWork Order / Work Request — the job number a ticket is charged to.
POETProject · Organization · Expenditure · Task — the charging string that routes a cost (see Charging Guide).
T&ETime & Equipment — billed under T350, always at a stip factor of 1.0.
StipStipulation — the payment factor (multiplier) set by the work-window timing.
HVIHigh Value Item — e.g. rock removal; requires a photo with placard before backfill.
M&SMains & Services.
MRPMain Replacement Program — replacing leak-prone (CI/WI, ST) mains with PE.
DLRDaily Log Report — the per-day crew & equipment record (see the Field Log app).
